Cell Theory

  • A cell is the basic unit of structure and function in an organism.
  • All living things are made up of cells.
  • All cells come from preexisting cells.

Organelles

Structure Function
Cytoplasm The part of the cell which is outside of the nucleus.
Nucleus Contains all DNA.
Nuclear envelope Surrounds nucleus. Is a membrane that allows things to pass through it.
Nucleolus In nucleus. Makes ribosomes.
Ribosome Parts of RNA and protein that make proteins.
Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum Makes lipids
Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum Makes proteins.
Golgi apparatus Modifies, sorts, and packages things from endoplasmic reticulum.
Lysosome Contains enzymes that help to break down molecules and organelles.
Vacuole Storage.
Mitochondria Create energy via cellular respiration.
Chloroplasts Create energy via photosynthesis.
Cytoskeleton Organizes cell.
Cillia or Flagella Movement.
Cell wall Provides support and protection.
Cell plasma membrane Membrane that is selectively permeable. Is a barrier for some cells.

Membranes

Basic Terms

solvent
a substance that something is dissolved into.
solute
what is being dissolved into the solvent.
diffusion
the process by which a substance moves across a membrane.
equilibrium
when the concentration of the solute is consistent.
isotonic
when both solutions are the same strength.
hypotonic
when one solution is below strength.
hypertonic
when a solution is above strength.
osmosis
the diffusion of water.
facilitated diffusion
diffusion that does not use energy. Osmosis is an example of this.

Active Transport

This is diffusion that does require energy, for it goes against the concentration gradient. Examples are:

endocytosis
taking in material by using the cell membrane.
phagocytosis
taking in material with extensions of the cell membrane.
pinocytosis
tiny pockets on the cell membrane fill with liquid and pinch off to form vacuoles in the cell.
exocytosis
the vacuole fuses with the membrane and forces materials out of the cell.

Additional Notes

Eukaryotic cells have a nucleus that contains genetic information, while the genetic information in a prokaryotic cell is not in the nucleus.
One of the major differences between plant cells and animal cells is that plant cells have chloroplasts and cell walls, while animal cells do not.
