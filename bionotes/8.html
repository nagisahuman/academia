ATP

Photosynthesis

Chloroplast Structure

Chloroplasts contain membranes called thylakoids. These thylakoids are arranged in stacks called grana. Photosystems, which absorb light, are located in the thylakoid membranes. The region outside of the thylakoids is the stroma.

Light Rx

  • The photosystems absorb light. This light is used to break apart water molecules.
  • Photosystem II turns ADP into ATP
  • Photosystem I turns NADP+ into NADPH
  • INPUT: light, H2O, ADP, NADP+
  • OUTPUT: oxygen, ATP, NADPH

Calvin Cycle

  • Takes place in the stroma.
  • Does not need light.
  • INPUT: CO2, NADPH, ATP
  • OUTPUT: ADP, NADP+, C6H12O6
