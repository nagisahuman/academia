Excretion

PATHWAY: Kidneys → ureter → urinary bladder → urethra → outside

The kidneys are important for filtration, secretion, reabsorbtion, and excretion. They do not filter plasma proteins.

Circulatory

SYSTEMIC:pulmonary veins → left atrium → left ventricle → aorta. This is to bring oxygen rich blood to the body.

PULMONARY:vena cava → right atrium → right ventricle → pulmonary arteries. This is to bring oxygen poor blood to the lungs.

Lymphatic

This collects what leaks from the circulatory system.

Blood

Blood composition:

  • 45&percent; cells
  • 55&percent; plasma. Plasma is made up of 90&percent; water, and 10&percent; other things. These other things include plasma proteins. Examples of plasma proteins include albumins, globulins, and fibrogens.

Types of cells:

  • White blood cells are defensive
  • Red blood cells transport nutrients
  • Platelets are important for blood clotting

Respiratory

PATHWAY:nose or mouth → pharynx → larynx → trachea → lungs → bronchi → bronchioles → alvioli

The purpose of this system is to bring oxygen in, and carbon dioxide out. This is controlled by the Medulla Oblongata, which monitors carbon dioxide levels.
The diaphragm contracts during inhalation, and relaxes when exhaling.
For air to be used by the body, it mst be warm, moist, and filtered.
