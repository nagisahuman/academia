Overview

Shape

  • Bacilli are shaped like rods.
  • Spirillus are shaped like spirals.
  • Coccus are shaped like spheres.

Energy

  • Heterotrophs
    • Chemoheterotrophs use organic molecules for energy and carbon.
    • Photoheterotrophs use photosynthesis for energy and organic compounds for carbon.
  • Autotrophs
    • Chemoheterotrophs use chemical reactions for energy.
    • Photoautorophs use photosynthesis for energy.

Relation with oxygen

  • Obligate aerobes require oxygen to survive.
  • Facultative anaerobes can survive with or without oxygen.
  • Obligate anaerobes are killed by oxygen.

Survival

  • Binary fission is when a cell creates a copy of itself.
  • Conjugation is when genes are transferred between cells.
  • Spores are when DNA is enclosed and remains dormant.

Viruses

Structure

A virus is typically made up of DNA or RNA that is surrounded by a protein coat. This protein is called a capsid.

Infection

A bacteriophage infects other cells. It inserts the DNA into the cell. This will cause one of two infections to happen: lyctic, or lysogenic. Lyctic infections occur when the viral DNA rapidly reproduces and eventually causes the cell to burst, causing many more bacteriophages to exist. Lysogenic infections are when the viral DNA inserts itself into the cell DNA. After a while, the DNA will exit that DNA, and a lyctic infection will occur.
