Meiosis

Meiosis is a process which creates gametes, AKA sex cells. These cells have half the usual number of chromosomes. They will be fertilized with another gamete to create a zygote, which will have the full number of chromosomes.

Process

  • Interphase 1 ‐ chromosomes duplicate
  • Prophase 1 ‐ chromosomes cross over with corresponding chromosomes. This causes them to exchange some of the alleles, producing a new combination of alleles.
  • Metaphase 1 ‐ chromosomes attach to spindle
  • Anaphase 1 ‐ chromosomes move to cell poles
  • Telophase 1 and Cytokinesis ‐ cell divides into two cells. Each of these cells will undergo the rest of meiosis.
  • Prophase 2 through end of Meiosis 2 ‐ normal mitosis happens. Overall, four genetically different daughter cells are formed.

Mendel

Gregor Mendel was a monk who was essentially the founding father of genetics, which is known as the scientific study of how characteristics are passed down between organisms.

trait
a specific characteristic
gene
a chemical factor that determines a trait
alleles
the different forms of a gene
segregation
the separation of alleles

Law of Segregation

Alleles separate during gamete formation so that gametes will only carry one copy of a gene.

Law of Independent Assortment

Genes segregate independently of each other.

Punnet Squares

Dominant trait
will always be represented. Symbolized by a capital letter.
Recessive trait
will only be represented if both copies of an allele are present. Symbolized by a lowercase letter.
Homozygous
if an organism has two of the same allele. Both letters are capital, or both letters are lowercase.
Heterozygous
if an organism has two different alleles. One letter is capital, while the other is lowercase.
Phenotype
the physical characteristic.
Genotype
the genetic makeup.

More Genetics

incomplete dominance
when neither allele is completely dominant. This creates a phenotype between the two alleles.
codominance
when both alleles contribute to the phenotype. This means that both alleles can be seen clearly.
multiple alleles
if a gene has more than two alleles in the population.
polygenic traits
traits that are controlled by more than one gene.
