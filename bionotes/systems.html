Systems

System Structures Functions
Nervous brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves Recognizes and determines body response to internal and external changes
Integumentary skin, hair, nails, sweat glands, oil glands Barrier and body temperature regulation
Respiratory nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi, bronchioles, lungs Oxygen intake, CO2 released
Digestive mouth, pharynx, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum Converts food into usable molecules, absorbs food
Excretory skin, lungs, kidneys, ureter, urinary bladder, urethra Eliminates waste and maintains homeostasis
Skeletal bones, cartilage, ligaments, tendons supports and protects body, helps movement
Muscular skeletal muscle, cardiac muscle, smooth muscle movement. blood and molecule circulation
Endocrine hypothalamus, pituitary, thyroid, parathyroids, adrenals, pancreas. ovaries or testes. Growth, metabolism, homeostasis
Reproductive ‐ Male testes, epididymis, vas deferens, urethra, penis produces reproductive cells
Reproductive ‐ Female ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, vagina produces reproductive cells. nurtures and protects embryo
lymphatic/immune white blood cells, thymus, spleen, lymph nodes, lymph vessels protect body from disease. returns fluids to circulatory system
