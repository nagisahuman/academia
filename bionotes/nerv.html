Overview

The nervous system controls and coordinates the body's functions. This system also responds to internal and external stimuli.

Neurons

Neurons are cells that transmit impulses. These cells make up the nervous system. The impulses are electrical signals that act as messages between cells. There are three types of neurons:

  • Sensory neurons ‐ carry impulses from sense organs to the spinal cord and brain.
  • Motor neurons ‐ carry impulses from the brain and spinal cord to muscles and glands.
  • Interneurons ‐ connect motor and sensory neurons, and carry impulses between these neurons.

Structure

There are dendrites which carry impulses to the cell body. The axon carries impulses away from the cell body. Most cells have a single axon, but multiple dendrites.

Nerve Impulse

While a neuron is at rest, the outside of the neuron has a positive charge, and the inside of the neuron has a negative charge. The inside of a cell has more potassium ions and fewer sodium ions. The ions leaking through membranes produces these charges.
The resting potential is the electrical charge of the cell membrane when the neuron is at rest.

The nerve impulse begins when a neuron is stimulated by another neuron or the environment. Sodium ions begin to flow into the cell, reversing the resting potential, therefore creating a positive charge. The reversal of charges is action potential.

The threshold is the minimum level of a stimulus needed to activate a neuron. If the level of stimulus is not high enough, or does not pass the threshold, the neuron will not be activated and no impulse will be transmitted.

Synapse

The synapse is the place where the neuron transfers an impulse to another cell. The axon terminal reaches the cell, but does not directly touch it. Neurotransmitters are released and diffuse into cell.

Divisions of the Nervous System

Central Nervous System

The CNS is made up of the brain and the spinal cord. The CNS will relay messages, process information, and analyze information. The cerebrum controls voluntary body activities, and is the site of intelligence, judgement, and learning. The cerebellum is coordinates muscle movements to be graceful and efficient. The brain stem regulates the information flow between the brain and the body. Finally, the spinal cord controls reflexes.

Peripheral Nervous System

The peripheral nervous system is made up of the nerves and cells which are not the brain and spinal cord. This includes cranial and spinal nerves.

Autonomic Nervous System

This regulates involuntary actions, such as those of blood vessels, glands, and internal organs. It includes the sympathetic nervous system and the parasympathetic nervous system, which work opposite of each other.

Somatic Nervous System

This system regulates voluntary actions, and uses the skeletal muscles.
