Adjectives

Degree

In Latin, adjectives are positive, comparative, or superlative. This is how they are in English:

Positive Comparative Superlative
tall taller tallest
smart smarter smartest
beautiful more beautiful most beautiful

In Latin, most adjectives use ‐ior‐ for the comparative form, and ‐issim‐ for their superlative form. Here are some examples:

Positive Comparative Superlative
forte fortior fortissimus
stultam stultiorem stultissimam

Prepositions

A preposition essentially describes a noun, normally to show location. The noun will take either the accusative or ablative case, depending on the preposition. Below is a preposition chart. Notice that in has a different meaning if the noun is accusative or ablative.

accusative ablative
in into in in, to
prope near cum with
ad to, towards sine without
per through sub below
apud among pro in front of
de down from, about
e, ex out of
a, ab from
