Present

  • Is happening now.
  • The best way to identify this is by knowing what it is.
  • It can also be identified through knowing that it is not imperfect or perfect.
  • Often follows these endings:
Singular Plural
I/we o mus
You s tis
He/She/It/They t nt

Examples:

  • ambulo ‐ I am walking
  • amat ‐ He loves

Conjugations

For first, second, and fourth conjugation verbs, use the second principle part ‐ the infinitive ‐, and remove the re.

  • First conjugation:stem + present endings. This only changes with first person singular, in which the first principle part is the first person singular.
  • Second conjugation: stem + present endings
  • Fourth conjugation: stem + present endings. This only changes with the third person plural, in which the ending is but ‐unt.

For the third conjugation verbs, the stem is found by removing the ere, and adding this endings before the present endings:

  • First person singular: o
  • Second person singular & plural, first person plural, and third person singular: i
  • Third person plural: u

Imperfect

  • Was happening
  • Can be identified by ‐ba‐ and imperfect ending
Singular Plural
First m mus
Second s tis
Third t nt

Conjugations

For first, second, and third conjugation verbs, the stem is found by removing the re. The verbs are all stem + ba + imperfect endings.
For fourth conjugation verbs, the stem is found by removing the ire from the infinitive. The verb is found by taking the stem + eba + imperfect endings.

Perfect

Endings

Singular Plural
First i imus
Second isti istis
Third it erunt

Conjugations

The stem is found by taking the third principle part and removing the i. The verb is formed by taking the stem and adding the perfect ending.

Pluperfect

Conjugations

The stem is found by taking the third principle part and removing the i. The verb is formed by adding the imperfect form of esse.
