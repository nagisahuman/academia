Nominative

Nouns in the nominative case are the subject of the sentence.
Examples are in bold.

  • Caecilius est fortis. Caecilius is brave.
  • Bregans ambulat. Bregans is walking.
  • ancillae perīre potest. The slave–girls can die.

Genitive

Nouns of the genitive case show possession. The genetive case also shows what declension a noun belongs to.

Dative

Nouns in the dative case are the indirect object in the sentence. These are normally translated with for or to.

  • Grumio mercatori cenam paravit. Grumio prepared dinner for the merchant.

Accusative

Nouns in the accusative case are the direct object in the sentence.

  • Bregans per vias ambulat. Bregans is walking through the streets.
  • Metella interficere potest Caecilium. Metella can kill Caecilius
  • rex vult canem. The king wants a dog.
